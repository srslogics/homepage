Overview

A clear start improves delivery quality.

SrS Logics builds software around the way a business actually runs. This packet explains how the delivery will move, what the client side should keep ready, how approvals are managed, and what is typically included by the time the system reaches handover.

01 Engagement model

Founder-led delivery with direct business understanding and software shaped around the client’s actual operating process.

02 Timeline window

Most engagements move from requirement gathering to testing within roughly 4 to 6 weeks, depending on complexity and response flow.

03 Support window

Post-handover maintenance and support are generally included for 6 months unless revised in writing for the engagement.

04 Commercial structure

Payments are usually handled in three phases: contract confirmation, API/testing milestone, and final handover.

Operating model

How the engagement will work.

Delivery approach

Our delivery model is built around business requirements, not generic software assumptions.

  • Build scope stays tied to the approved workflow and operating needs.
  • Client response time affects delivery speed, especially during review and testing phases.
  • The best outcomes happen when process owners remain involved in milestone reviews.

Commercial and support principles

Commercial and support clarity are treated as part of delivery discipline, not as last-stage paperwork.

  • Pricing depends on system speed expectations, data flow, and local or cloud infrastructure decisions.
  • New items beyond approved scope should be logged separately to avoid confusion and drift.
  • Support remains structured after handover so the client team is not left unsupported.
Timeline

Typical delivery journey.

Stage Typical timing What happens Client role
01 Week 1 Requirement understanding, operating flow review, scope alignment, and approval of the working plan. Share workflow, decision makers, and priority outcomes.
02 Week 2 Interface structure, process definition, database planning, and internal build setup. Confirm fields, process details, and exception cases.
03 Weeks 3-4 Core development, logic implementation, reporting flow, and platform assembly. Review milestone previews and respond quickly to clarification points.
04 Weeks 4-6 Testing, API validation, issue fixes, deployment preparation, and structured handover. Participate in testing and prepare final users for go-live.

Handover

What closure typically includes.

Typical handover package

  • Approved software build aligned to the agreed scope
  • Deployment-ready environment and relevant database structure
  • Role handling, access logic, and operating instructions where required
  • Testing closure notes and final issue summary
  • Defined support window after handover

Shared responsibility creates better software.

This welcome kit is meant to create a better working rhythm before development begins. When scope, approvals, testing, and handover are handled clearly from the start, the software becomes more stable, more useful, and more aligned with the way the business actually operates.
