Business context

Dual-side operational systems work best when management visibility, field-side reporting, and public communication are treated as one connected delivery.

This engagement required one side of the system to support owner-level control, another to remain practical for farmer-side daily reporting, and a public-facing website to represent the business more clearly. The structure was designed to improve process visibility, software fit, and information handling across all three layers.

Core objective

Translate a specialized business requirement into a usable operational system that supports owner-side control, farmer-side execution, and a stronger public-facing presence in real daily use.

What the system needed to support
  • Role-specific process logic The software needed to support different operating needs across owner workflows, farmer workflows, and the outward-facing website rather than force one generic model.
  • Internal visibility Make workflow movement, updates, and reporting easier to understand and review from the owner side while keeping the public-facing layer clearer.
  • Practical field usage Keep the farmer-facing flow simple enough for real reporting use without reducing the usefulness of the management side.
How SrS Logics approached it
  • Requirement-specific structure The software was built around the client’s actual operational requirement rather than borrowed from a generic industry package.
  • On-site reliability The live phase focuses on making sure the system behaves reliably under real operating scenarios and day-to-day reporting flow.
  • Practical delivery mindset The system is being shaped for operational usefulness, not just technical completion.
Observed result

A more specialized operational platform now being used on-site for steadier client-side usage.

On-site

The system is now in live client use rather than remaining in testing-only condition.

Role-led

Practical

The present focus is on operational reliability and fit during ongoing real use.
