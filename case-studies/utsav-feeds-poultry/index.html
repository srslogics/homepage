Owner and farmer app delivery for Utsav

Industry-specific software needs to reflect the way the business actually operates. This engagement centers on building a more dependable operational system for owner-side visibility and farmer-side reporting, with the platform now in on-site use.

Business context

Dual-side operational systems work best when both management visibility and field-side reporting are treated seriously.

This engagement required one side of the system to support owner-level control while the other side remained practical for farmer-side daily reporting. The structure was designed to improve process visibility, software fit, and information handling across both roles.

Core objective

Translate a specialized business requirement into a usable operational system that supports both owner-side control and farmer-side execution in real daily use.

What the system needed to support
  • Role-specific process logic The software needed to support different operating needs across owner and farmer workflows rather than force one generic screen model.
  • Internal visibility Make workflow movement, updates, and reporting easier to understand and review from the owner side.
  • Practical field usage Keep the farmer-facing flow simple enough for real reporting use without reducing the usefulness of the management side.
How SrS Logics approached it
  • Requirement-specific structure The software was built around the client’s actual operational requirement rather than borrowed from a generic industry package.
  • On-site reliability The live phase focuses on making sure the system behaves reliably under real operating scenarios and day-to-day reporting flow.
  • Practical delivery mindset The system is being shaped for operational usefulness, not just technical completion.
Observed result

A more specialized operational platform now being used on-site for steadier client-side usage.

On-site

The system is now in live client use rather than remaining in testing-only condition.

Role-led

The delivery is shaped around different owner and farmer requirements instead of a generic one-screen model.

Practical

The present focus is on operational reliability and fit during ongoing real use.
